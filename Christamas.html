Irish Christmas Holidays

An overview of traditions and celebrations in Ireland.

Christmas Eve (Oíche Nollag) - December 24

  • Religious Tradition: Many families attend Midnight Mass, symbolizing the start of Christmas.
  • Preparation: Homes are decorated, and Christmas trees and nativity scenes are completed.
  • Candles in the Window: A large, often red candle is placed in the window as a symbol of hospitality.
  • Food: Light meals, such as seafood like smoked salmon or prawns, are served.

Christmas Day (Lá Nollag) - December 25

  • Morning: Gifts are exchanged, and children open presents from Santa Claus. Some attend morning church services.
  • Main Celebration: A lavish Christmas dinner with turkey, ham, and traditional sides. Desserts like Christmas pudding or trifle are highlights.
  • Tradition: In coastal areas, some brave people take a cold morning swim in the sea for charity or as a challenge.

St. Stephen's Day (Lá Fhéile Stiofán) - December 26

  • Public Holiday: Similar to Boxing Day, it is spent relaxing or visiting relatives.
  • Wren Day: In rural areas, there are parades where groups in costumes go door to door singing and performing.
  • Sports: Many people attend horse races or watch Gaelic football.

Little Christmas (Nollaig na mBan) - January 6

  • Significance: The official end of the Christmas season, also known as "Women's Christmas."
  • Tradition: Women are celebrated, and men take over household duties.
  • Decorations: Christmas decorations are removed, as keeping them up after this date is considered bad luck.
